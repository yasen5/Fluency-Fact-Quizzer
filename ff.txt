Columbian Exchange
Who
Mainly Spaniards initiated, affected Native Americans but also the Old World due to the plants brought back and the new fertile land being used to supply those crops.
Why
Arrival of spaniards and other explorers seeking wealth. 
What
Spaniards and other European explorers transferred deadly diseases to Native Americans which decimated their populations. They also brought domesticated animals like horses which, alongside new metal tools, revolutionized many Native Americans' ways of life. On the other side of the exchange, Europeans got new crops like the potato and tobacco for nutrition and recreation respectively. 
Where
Americas, Europe, China, Africa
When
1492-1500s
Significance
New crops greatly increased life expectancy of the average European, and new lands to farm flooded the market with crops like sugar. Disease decimated the Native population, and animals and meat changed the Native way of life

Encomienda System
Who
Spaniards (conquistadors granted encomenderos), Native Americans in Spanish-controlled territories (Mexico, American SW), Bartolom√© de las Casas
Why
Exploitation of Native labor for profit. Spain also wanted more Spanish citizens in the Americas since they didn't have very good incentives for people to emigrate from Spain to the New World
What
Conquistadors granted encomiendas by the Crown, given rights to the labor of the indigenous people on that land (without their consent). In return Spanish were supposed to provide protection from other tribes, and convert indigenous people to Spanish culture and Catholicism. Limited in 1542 by the New Laws, which were supposed to free the indigenous people from enslavement and shift over to Africans. It also allowed indigenous people to settle internal disputes using their native customs, and they were granted protectors in court who could use Native traditions that didn't conflict with Catholicism.
Where
American SW (esp. New Mexico), Western coast of South America
When
1512-1720 (limited in 1542)
Significance
Led many Native Americans to die from disease (exposure to Europeans) and overworking. Led to resentment of Europeans and violent rebellions. Instilled ideas of race-based hierarchies that persisted after the collapse of the encomiendas.

Mexican-American War
Who
Santa Anna, Zachary Taylor, James Polk, Irish & German immigrants, Mexicans
Why
Manifest Destiny Same as Manifest Destiny
What
Zachary Taylor, at command of James Polk, incites conflict with Mexico. U.S. goes to war, heavy casualties due to disease, desertion rampant (esp. among immigrants, saw the horrors and left or even switched sides). Many accusations of unjust war, but even antiwar Whigs don't oppose it strongly enough to stop voting to continue it. Americans commit atrocities, shell civilians and commit sexual violence, eventually capture Mexico City and force the sale of California, New Mexico, Nevada, Colorado, Utah, Arizona
Where
New Mexico, California, Nevada, Colorado, Utah, Arizona, Texas, Mexico City
When
1846-1848
Significance
Same as Manifest destiny + revealed that Americans wouldn't be automatically patriotic if the war was unjust, exposed powerful voices like Abraham Lincoln and Frederick Douglas

Know-Nothing Party
Who
Middle, working class, "Conscience Whigs"
Why
Feared job competition
What
A third political party (although Whig power was already waning), semi-secret organization, strongly opposed immigrants (esp. Chinese) and Catholics. Wanted 21 year naturalization process, forced deportation of beggars & criminals, mandatory bible reading in schools, elimination of all Catholics from public office. Had some success getting elected but didn't get legislation. Dissipated with the arrival of the Republican Party 1854
Where
US
When
Late 1840s-early 1850s
Significance
Kind of a 3rd political party, even though they didn't last long, their sentiments can be seen in the harsh anti-Immigration policies, also the KKK

Compromise of 1850
Who
Henry Clay
Why
Preservation of the Union over the issue of slavery
What
Five things:
California admitted as free state
Utah, New Mexico given popular sovereignty for slavery (North not too       concerned, land isn't great for farming anyway
Texas got $10M to give up its land claims in New Mexico
Fugitive slave act, allowed federal agents to forcibly relocate African Americans suspected of being escaped slaves
Slave trade ended in Washington D.C., slavery itself persists
Where
U.S.
When
1850
Significance
Last attempt made at real compromise, pure downhill from here. Fugitive slave act a big mistake, put the brutality of slavery in the minds of even moderate Northerners and turned them abolitionist once people started getting dragged through the streets. Also made the Northerners dissatisfied with the government, believed it was too sympathetic to slavery

Kansas-Nebraska Act
Who
Stephen Douglas, John Calhoun, Henry Clay, Daniel Webster
Why
Stephen Douglas wanted to build some railroad that out west that would link his property holdings to strong transportation infrastructure. Needed population in the Western territories to get this to happen, needed an event that would cause people to rush in.
What
Douglas somehow managed to get this through, it violated the Missouri Compromise because it allowed two more slave states in territory above the 36th parallel. Unlike NM and Utah these areas were actually good for farming, so there was the perceived threat of slavery. People rushed in and things even got violent in Bleeding Kansas. Both states admitted as free anyway because the pro-slavery constitution for Kansas was rejected.
Where
Kansas, Nebraska
When
1854
Significance
Showed that the Union had been brought to its breaking point, further broke down abolitionist trust in the government to contain slavery, split the Whig and Democrat parties down geographical lines and shifted everything to be about slavery.

Dred Scott Decision
Who
Supreme Court (Judge Taney), Dred Scott obv
Why
Taney extremely racist ig?
What
Dred Scott sues for freedom after being brought to a free state, Taney decided that African Americans could never be citizens, violating the usual situation in Northern states, and thus making it so that Scott didn't even have the right to sue for his freedom. He also stated that enslaved people were property and thus the constitution barred any state from banning it (Missouri Compromise struck down).
Where
Supreme Court
When
1857
Significance
One of the major reasons why the civil war occurred, major distrust between factions that each thought the other was taking over the government, abolitionists thought that if Southern slaveowners weren't stopped then and there, their influence would spread throughout the government

Emancipation Proclamation
Who
Lincoln, Confederate States
Why
To get African American soldiers in the army, make the war about slavery so that European countries couldn't ally with Confederates even though they depended on Southern cotton.
What
Happened following the Battle of Antietam (1862) which gave Lincoln a breather after the string of Union losses to issue his proclamation. Stated that all slaves in the Southern territories were property of people in rebellion and thus could be confiscated and freed. Didn't free anyone immediately, also held no promises for emancipation in the 4 border states that held slaves but weren't in rebellion, Lincoln didn't feel like he had the constitutional right to take property from people he wasn't at war with.
Where
U.S.
When
1862-1863
Significance
Finally got some abolitionist support (although they criticized how it didn't ban slavery outright), got 200K African American soldiers over time to join the Union Army, part of the Union advantage over the South. 

Homestead Act
Who
Republican Congress, Lincoln
Why
To fill in the territories recently acquired during Westward Expansion.
What
Offered 160 acres for basically nothing, under the condition that people would develop the land and live there for 5 years. However many people who took the deal were from lower classes trying to escape their situation and weren't prepared for the farming lifestyle, so they left their claims early. Much of the land fell to land speculators and railroad companies.
Where
West
When
1862
Significance
Made the Civil War at least partly about Western Expansion, army was employed against Native Americans. Also filled out some new territories, important for future elections. Since it was passed by the North, anyone could take it including single women and African Americans

Gettysburg Address
Who
Lincoln
Why
Boost morale
What
Lincoln talks about how preserving the Union is of the utmost importance, cannot let the exemplary democratic nation founded on liberty fall. Also mentions that it is a fight for equality "all men are created equal." States that the soldiers died doing the right thing and that they should be honored
Where
Gettysburg, PA
When
1863
Significance
Establishes the main issues of the Civil War, why it is so important that the North should win, otherwise it proves that democracies cannot last because secession will break them apart.

Reconstruction Amendments
Who
Radical Republicans, former slaves
Why
Transform the South to be more like the north
What
13th (1865): Abolished slavery, 14th (1868): Birthright citizenship, equal due process of law, 15th (1870): Banned barring voting based on race, gender, etc. Got bypassed 
Where
Whole U.S., but mostly South
When
1865-1870
Significance
Finally outlawed discrimination in the constitution, even if it wasn't really followed in the South due to Jim Crow. Federal gov responsible for protecting votes, not states

Sharecropping
Who
Freed slaves, southern landowners
Why
Retain slavery and previous economic system in the South
What
African Americans were landless, offered jobs by White racist landowners, didn't have many options so the landowners were able to sell them tools at high prices and interest rates which kept them perpetually in debt, so they had to keep promising more and more of their share of crops to the landowner.
Where
South
When
Late 1860s-1940s
Significance
Meant that the promise of freedom for slaves wasn't really real, many African Americans became disillusioned because they couldn't escape their lower economic tier. Caused many to move west where they could get land under the Homestead Act.

Gilded Age
Who
Upper class, wage laborers, farmers, Mark Twain, Rockefeller, Carnegie
Why
Rise of American industry, lack of proper regulation
What
Companies bought up competitors (horizontal integration) and bought up their suppliers (vertical integration). Lead to monopolies like Rockefeller controlling 90% of the oil. Railroad monopolies especially restricted the profits of farmers and sank them into debt.
Where
U.S.
When
1860-1900
Significance
Modern cities, horrible conditions for tenement laborers, child labor common, monopolies allow further exploitation. Backlash on this leads to the rise of populist parties that take advantage of workers' frustration to get a fair portion of the vote.

Who
Andrew Cargnegie
Why
Justify his massive wealth and mistreatment of workers, why it is okay to not regulate anything
What
Heavy Social Darwinist influences, idea that the fittest will rise to the top and not only deserve their wealth, but are also the only ones capable of properly handling that wealth. He therefore thinks that after accumulating all his money, simply giving some of it away would make up for the horrific conditions in his factories.
Where
U.S.
When
1889
Significance
Influences Rockefeller and Ford. Wanted to promote Laissez Faire economics, make people trust the wealthy to handle wealth rather than using progressive taxation to move it about. "trickle down" economics.

Dawes Severalty Act
Who
Congress, Grover Cleveland, Henry Dawes (senator)
Why
Supposedly for assimilation of Native Americans, converting them to farming culture
What
Split up traditionally communal lands into individual plots. This divided the tribe and caused disunity, after which Whites were able to buy up land and further reduce tribe resources. Also the land sucked for farming, and NAs who had been hunter/gatherers were inexperienced in farming, so they struggled and lost more land as a result of predatory economic practices.
Where
NA Reservations
When
1887
Significance
Further continued the trend of decreasing tribal power, cultural extermination. Led to adoption of policies like boarding schools (Carlisle)

Why

What
Attempted arrest of Sitting Bull, a chieftain, for participating in Ghost Dance movement turns into a murder. Then the army was confiscating weapons from other Dancers and ended up just massacring a bunch of noncombatants including unarmed women and children.
Where
South Dakota
When
1890
Significance
Ended the Ghost Dance movement.

American Federation of Labor
Who
Samuel Gompers, White male workers
Why
Better working conditions
What
Very large union, did not allow female or immigrant workers to join. Wasn't very political, largely just peaceful mediation for higher wages, better conditions, shorter hours. Organized along craft lines, like a guild. 
Where
U.S.
When
1886-1955 (technically never ended since it merged with CIO)
Significance
Longest-lasting union we learned about, didn't suffer major setbacks like the Haymarket Affair that destroyed other unions like the KoL. Took up the KoL's fight but dropped African Americans and women.
